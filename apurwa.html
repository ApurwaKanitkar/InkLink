Connections Worth Treasuring

Apurwa Kanitkar | 2 min read | 07 Aug 2023

Every friendship day brings to life a dreamscape of happy faces tying friendship bands, celebrating the self-chosen family. It is a relation that helps us navigate complex landscapes in the odyssey of life. Be it the occasions of euphoric bliss or moments that dampen the spirit, a true friend always stands by us. Right from childhood, we live the phrases like — “sharing is caring” and “a friend in need is a friend indeed”. Through friends, we learn to form connections, grow, and evolve individually. As we grow older, the frequency of those coveted gatherings and leisurely phone calls reduces to some extent, buoyed by busy schedules. But the bonds with firm foundations stand the test of time and become stronger. When friends meet, even after countless days, the depth and affection remain unaltered, as — “True friends are never apart, maybe in distance but never in heart!”.

Friendship transcends the shackles of socio-economic status. It is well evident in the exemplary bond between lord Krishna and Sudama. Lord Krishna belonged to a royal background, while Sudama was born in a poor household. They went different ways after studying together during childhood. Nevertheless, they did not leave hopes of reuniting. The epitome of virtue, Sudama, remained dedicated and devoted to his beloved friend Krishna. When Sudama visited Lord Krishna after years of being apart, it was a poignant reunion. The guards of the palace laughed him away, looking at his tattered clothes. But Lord Krishna, on hearing about his friend’s arrival, rushed to the gates & hugged him. He invited Sudama inside, washed his feet, and happily ate the modest food that Sudama had brought for him. Such a pristine friendship is seldom seen and continues to inspire generations.

Another such saga is that of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj and Subedar Tanhaji Malusare. They were close comrades since their youth. Their tale is a pinnacle of loyalty and trust. When the Kondhana fort was captured by the Mughals, Tanhaji undertook the mammoth mission of recapturing it, despite the occasion of his son’s wedding falling during the same time. Staying true to his words, he made sure that the Maratha flag stood tall at the fort but lost his life in the process. To honor his sacrifice and valor, Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj renamed the fort — “Sinhagad”, meaning “Lion’s Fort”. The local songs called powadas stand testimony to their story.

Life is an exhilarating rollercoaster ride to be enjoyed with a bunch of friends. Some of them are a transient part of our journey while some stay till the very end. But each one plays a special and unique role of tremendous value. Undoubtedly, the only way to have a friend is to be one.
